# Que Media Intelligence Design Decisions

Date: 2026-07-20

## Working scene

A Que Media strategist uses this product at a desk in a bright office, usually with several research tabs open and limited time before choosing the next business to contact. The interface must stay calm and legible under heavy information, make evidence easy to verify, and keep the next best action visible without turning research into a sales pipeline.

## Direction

- Product register, responsive web platform.
- Dense but comfortable intelligence workspace.
- Light editorial canvas from `DESIGN.md` with ink navy structure and cobalt reserved for the primary action, active navigation, and focus.
- Inter carries all controls, body copy, data, and labels. Newsreader provides the humanist serif italic accent for major page titles only.
- 4px and 8px spacing rhythm derived from the supplied 8px system, with 12px available for product-density grouping.
- Cards appear only for distinct actionable objects. Tables, ruled sections, split panes, and open canvas group the rest.
- Scores are always paired with labels, explanations, and evidence access. Color never carries status alone.
- Motion is crisp and state-based: 150ms press, 90ms release, 160ms pointer-only color feedback, and 220ms drawers. Keyboard state changes are immediate. No page-load choreography.
- The shipped workspace contains no fabricated records. Empty states carry the product until verified integrations or user-created information exists.

## Information architecture

1. Briefing: prioritized opportunity queue, timely signals, research coverage, and agent activity.
2. Discover: searchable, filterable Ottawa business universe with transparent qualification factors.
3. Company workspace: overview, social intelligence, website and local audit, competitors, strategy, outreach, and relationship history.
4. Reports: client-ready audit previews with print and PDF-ready styling.
5. Research agents: specialist status, current work, evidence quality, and manual rerun controls.
6. Sources: connector health, access boundaries, refresh policies, and compliance state.

## Product signatures

- Evidence drawer: any consequential score or claim opens its supporting observations, contradictions, freshness, and source type.
- Why Now rail: time-bounded triggers are presented as a reasoned brief, not an urgency badge.
- Content DNA: public posts are classified into useful formats and tied to hooks, production choices, audience reactions, and missing opportunities.
- Opportunity equation: fit, opportunity, timing, reachability, confidence, and effort remain visible instead of collapsing into one opaque number.
- Manual-only outreach studio: drafts are editable and copyable, but there is no send control.

## Responsive behavior

- Desktop: persistent 248px sidebar, wide evidence tables, split-pane company workspace.
- Tablet: collapsible navigation, two-column summary layouts, horizontally scrollable comparison tables with visible labels.
- Mobile: single-column hierarchy, compact top bar, bottom navigation for primary areas, progressive disclosure for evidence and audits.
- Print: navigation and interactive controls disappear, long sections expand, sources remain visible, and charts use print-safe fills and labels.

## Explicit rejections

- Generic equal-card dashboard grids.
- Hero metrics used as decoration.
- Gradient text, glass surfaces, crypto glows, or background grid effects.
- Automatic outreach, send buttons, campaigns, sequences, or mailbox controls.
- Social metrics presented without access level or source provenance.
- Empty states that do not explain the next useful action.
- Seeded companies, invented research activity, placeholder evidence, or fabricated performance metrics.
