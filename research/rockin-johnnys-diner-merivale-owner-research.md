## Executive finding

Rockin' Johnny's has reopened the former Westgate diner operation at **1651 Merivale Road**, in the former Happy Greek space near No Frills, Winners, and Value Village. The Ottawa Business Journal reported the planned address and an early-the-following-week opening on July 10, 2026. A local diner reported that it opened on **July 15, 2026**, and another reported eating there on July 18. These eyewitness reports make July 15 the best available opening date, but it is not yet confirmed by an official announcement.

Ownership must be recorded by role rather than flattened into one unqualified "owner" field:

- **Enzo Mastromattei** is identified by two independent Ottawa publications as the **franchise owner**. He opened the original Westgate Rockin' Johnny's in 1991 and is the strongest-supported brand/franchise principal.
- **Anthony Joseph** bought the Westgate location in **2005** and is portrayed as its hands-on location owner/operator. The reporting says Mastromattei and Joseph jointly chose the new Merivale site.
- The exact current legal entity operating the Merivale restaurant, its directors/shareholders, and the precise equity relationship between Mastromattei and Joseph remain **unverified**. Do not describe either person as the sole legal owner without an Ontario Business Registry profile or equivalent primary record.

The official Rockin' Johnny's website is stale as of this review: it says Westgate is closed and lists only the Kanata location. It does not yet list Merivale, its hours, telephone, delivery links, or an opening announcement.

## Ownership analysis

### Strongly supported

1. Ottawa Business Journal calls **Enzo Mastromattei the franchise owner** and says he opened the Westgate restaurant in 1991.
2. The same report says Mastromattei and **Anthony Joseph, who bought the Westgate location in 2005**, selected the Merivale site.
3. Kitchissippi Times independently uses the same role distinction: Joseph bought the location in 2005, while Mastromattei is the franchise owner searching for a replacement site.
4. The official website corroborates the brand history: the first Rockin' Johnny's opened at Westgate in June 1991.

### Historical legal-name evidence

Canadian trademark records show **ROCKIN' JOHNNY'S DINER LTD.** participated as an opponent in a trademark proceeding that closed in 1999. A separate third-party trademark database records a 1996 Rockin' Johnny's application by that company as abandoned. This establishes a historical company name, not that it remains the active Merivale operator.

### Not established

- Current Ontario corporation number, status, directors, shareholders, or business-name registration
- Whether Mastromattei or a licensing company owns the current brand intellectual property
- Whether Joseph owns the Merivale restaurant personally or through a corporation
- Equity split or formal franchise/licensing terms
- A current liquor, food-premises, occupancy, or municipal business record tied to the new location
